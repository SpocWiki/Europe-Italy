## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> The Uffizi Gallery (UK:  yoo-FIT-see, uu-FEET-see; 
> Italian: Galleria degli Uffizi, pronounced [ɡalleˈriːa deʎʎ ufˈfittsi]) 
> is a prominent art museum adjacent to the Piazza della Signoria 
> in the Historic Centre of Florence in the region of Tuscany, Italy. 
> 
> One of the most important Italian museums and the most visited, 
> it is also one of the largest and best-known in the world 
> and holds a collection of priceless works, particularly from the period of the Italian Renaissance.
>
> After the ruling House of Medici died out, their art collections were given to the city of Florence 
> under the famous Patto di famiglia ("family pact") negotiated by Anna Maria Luisa, 
> the last Medici heiress. 
> 
> The Uffizi is one of the first modern museums. 
> The gallery had been open to visitors by request since the sixteenth century, 
> and in 1769 it was officially opened to the public, formally becoming a museum in 1865.
>
> [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uffizi)
